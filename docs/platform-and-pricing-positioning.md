# Platform and Pricing Positioning

This document is for public-facing messaging. Keep it accurate, conservative, and non-proprietary.

## Core Message

LegalSearch is an interoperability layer. It lets external agents ground basic Indian legal research in Roop's public knowledge layer without forcing users to migrate their stack.

Roop's platform is the native work environment. For ongoing professional legal work, it can be cheaper and more powerful because the agent, corpus, drafting, matter context, verification loops, collaboration, usage accounting, and user experience are integrated.

## What To Say

- "Use LegalSearch from your existing agent."
- "Start with public judgments and statutes without migrating your workflow."
- "For sustained legal work, Roop's native platform and Roop Agents remove the overhead of stitching external tools together."
- "The MCP is bounded for public corpus access; the platform is where deeper legal workflows live."

## What Not To Say

- Do not imply the MCP exposes private Roop platform internals.
- Do not promise legal advice or guaranteed legal correctness.
- Do not claim cost savings unless pricing has been approved and published.
- Do not disclose private architecture, private technical names, operational hostnames, or security controls beyond public documentation.
- Do not position external agents as inferior. Position Roop as the integrated home for heavier work.

## Pricing Principles

The public MCP should be priced so users understand the ladder:

1. Anonymous/free access supports discovery and small packets.
2. Paid MCP access supports external-agent grounding at professional scale.
3. Roop platform subscriptions should be economically attractive for users who repeatedly need research, drafting, verification, matter context, and collaboration.

Pricing pages should make the tradeoff obvious:

- External MCP: flexible, interoperable, best for adding Roop grounding to existing agents.
- Roop platform: integrated, workflow-native, best for sustained legal work.

## Suggested Public Wording

"LegalSearch is built for agent interoperability. You can connect Claude Code, Codex, Gemini agents, Hermes, Cursor, OpenClaw-style agents, and other MCP-capable tools to Roop's public legal knowledge layer. For high-volume or end-to-end legal work, Roop's native platform offers a more integrated and cost-efficient path through indigenous Roop Agents."
